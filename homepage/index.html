How To Reduce The Risk Of COVID 19?

Wear A Mask

Do wear a mask if you are feeling unwell, or going to crowded places such as shopping malls or taking the public transport.

Practice Social Distancing

Keep a distance of at least 1 metre apart between one another whenever possible.

Reduce Social Interactions

Try to limit your social interactions outside your home, limit to 2 family household per day.

Avoid Touching Your Face

Avoid touching your eyes, nose and mouth unnecessarily, especially when your hands are not clean.

Wash Your Hands Frequently

Washing your hands with either soap or hand sanitizer can be very effective in killings germs, viruses and bacteria

Get Vaccinated

Vaccination can help to protect you from severe disease and boost your immnunity against Covid-19!

What To Do If You Are Infected With COVID 19?

You Should:

Self Isolate for 72 Hours

You should isolate in a room where there is a toliet if possible. You should stay in your room and minimise interaction with your household members. After 72 hours, do the ART test. If you tested negative after 72 hours, you may end your isolation.

Monitor Your Symptoms

Check your symptoms regularly, such as fever, cough, difficulty breathing, loss of taste or smell. Do seek medical attention as soon as possible if your symptoms worsen over time.

Stay Hydrated and Rest

Drink plenty of water and get ample rest to support your recovery. Ensure you get enough sleep and take breaks if you need to support your immune system and fight against the virus.

You Should NOT:

Have Close Contact With Others

Avoid any contact with others, such as your family members, friends and others. This will help to reduce and slow down the transmission of Covid-19.

Share Personal Household Items

Avoid sharing any of your personal items with others, including household members, as it can help reduce the spread of germs and viruses, such as COVID-19. These include eating utensils, towels, cups, etc.

Leave Your House

If you are infected, try not to leave your house to go outside, such as to work, school, shopping malls, hawker centres and any other public places. Be socially responsible and stay at home. You should only leave your home if you need medical care.
